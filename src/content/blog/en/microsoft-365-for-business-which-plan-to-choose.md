Microsoft 365 is one of the best ways to give your business professional email,
Office and collaborative working. But with so many plans available, the question
arises: **which one is right for my company?** Here is a clear explanation
without jargon.

## First: do you need desktop applications?

The main difference between plans is whether you need **Office installed**
(Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Outlook on your computer) or whether the web
versions are enough.

- If your team works heavily with complex documents, a plan with desktop apps
  is usually the better choice.
- If usage is light, a more affordable plan with Office on the web may be
  sufficient.

## Email, storage and collaboration

Almost all plans include:

- **Professional email** with your domain and 50 GB mailboxes.
- **OneDrive** with 1 TB of storage per user.
- **Teams** for meetings and teamwork.

If email and collaboration are your priorities, there are plans designed
exactly for that — so you do not pay for features you do not use.

## Security: a factor you should not overlook

Some plans add **advanced security** features (threat protection, device
management, and more). For many SMEs, basic security is enough at the start,
but it is worth reviewing your needs depending on your sector.
